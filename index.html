Allama Muhammad Iqbal

1877 - 1938

Missile Man of Pakistan

Sir Muhammad Iqbal (9 November 1877 â€“ 21 April 1938) was a renowned philosopher, poet, and politician, widely regarded as one of the most important figures in Urdu literature. He was born in Sialkot, in present-day Pakistan, and studied at Government College Lahore, later pursuing higher education in philosophy in Cambridge, Germany, and London. Iqbal is credited with inspiring the idea of an independent Muslim state, which eventually led to the creation of Pakistan. His poetry in Urdu and Persian emphasized self-realization, spiritual awakening, and the revival of Islamic culture, earning him the title of "Mufakkir-e-Pakistan" (The Thinker of Pakistan) and "Shair-e-Mashriq" (The Poet of the East). He played a vital intellectual role in the All-India Muslim League and influenced the ideology behind the Pakistan Movement.
